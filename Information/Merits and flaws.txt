MERITS AND FLAWS
================================================================================

LINGUISTICS
Note: Each dot of Linguistics allows the character to read, write and speak
fluently in another language outside of their default two (native language +
language of the Domain).

  MERITS
  - Linguistics (• +)
    Each dot grants fluency in one additional language (read, write, speak).

  FLAWS
  - Illiterate (••)
    Cannot read or write. Science and Academics Skills may not exceed 1 dot.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

LOOKS
Related to appearance, supernaturally influenced or not.

  MERITS
  - Beautiful (••)
    Add one die to related Social pools.

  - Stunning (••••)
    Add two dice to related Social pools.

  - Semblance of the Methuselah (• – ••)
    Appearance strikingly similar to a methuselah. Gain one die on rolls to
    impress, intimidate or attract attention from those who recognize the face.
    May gain additional status or bonus dice when meeting the resembled methuselah.

  - Famous Face (•)
    Appear as someone famous. Gain two dice in social tests where this helps.
    Take a two-dice penalty when trying to hide in a crowd or avoid recognition.

  - Ingénue (•)
    Appear innocent and blameless. Add two dice to rolls related to avoiding
    suspicion or deflecting blame (Storyteller's discretion).

  - Remarkable Feature (•)
    Possess a rare, memorable feature (unusual eye color, complexion, etc.).
    Add two dice to social interactions with strangers.
    Take a one-die penalty to disguise yourself.

  - Up All Night (•• or ••••)
    Treat Humanity as one higher (max 10) when using Blush of Life, eating,
    drinking, or sexual intercourse. At four dots, treat as two higher.

  - Scene Kid (•)
    Style embodies a particular subculture. Add one die to all appropriate
    Social pools when dealing with that subculture.

  FLAWS
  - Ugly (•)
    Lose one die from related Social pools.

  - Repulsive (••)
    Lose two dice from related Social pools.

  - Stench (•)
    Supernaturally foul breath and body odor. Lose one die from seduction and
    similar Social pools. Lose two dice from Stealth pools unless upwind.

  - Transparent (•)
    Unable to lie due to terrible poker face or compulsion to be truthful.
    Lose one die in pools requiring Subterfuge. Cannot take any Subterfuge dots.

  - Unblinking Visage (••)
    Treat Humanity as two lower (min 0) when using Blush of Life, eating,
    drinking, or sexual intercourse.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

SUBSTANCE USE
Vampires seek prey with a specific drug in their system (defined at character
creation). Effects of specific substances still apply.

  MERITS
  - High Functioning Addict (•)
    Add one die to either Physical, Social, or Mental pools when the last
    feeding had the drug of their desire.

  FLAWS
  - Addiction (•)
    Lose one die to all pools if the last feeding was not on the drug of choice,
    unless the action is to immediately obtain that drug.

  - Hopeless Addiction (••)
    Lose two dice to all pools if the last feeding was not on the drug of choice,
    unless the action is to immediately obtain that drug.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

ARCHAIC
May only be taken by Ancilla or older vampires.

  MERITS
  - Custodian of History (•)
    Gain +1 to all relevant Skill tests pertaining to a chosen period or
    character in Kindred lore.

  FLAWS
  - Living in the Past (•)
    Unable to grasp modern society's mindset. One or more convictions reflect
    outdated views.

  - Archaic (••)
    Cannot use computers or cellphones. Technology rating is always 0.

  - Grief Phobia (•)
    Lose one die to all tests made while in the presence of a phobic stimulus
    linked to a traumatically lost Touchstone.

  - Old Tricks (•)
    All specialties must be Archaic in nature.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

BONDING
These can be combined to alter how the Blood Bond works.

  MERITS
  - Bond Resistance (• – •••)
    Add one die per level to resist Blood Bonds.

  - Short Bond (••)
    Bonds decrease by two levels each month if not reinforced.

  - Unbondable (•••••)
    Cannot be Blood Bonded.

  - Bonds of Fealty (•••)
    Dominate powers do not require eye contact on those bound to the character.
    Requires Dominate discipline.

  - Enduring Bond (•)
    Bonds the character creates last longer, weakening only every other month.

  FLAWS
  - Bond Junkie (•)
    The bond is sweeter to this character. Lose one die when acting against
    Blood Bonds.

  - Long Bond (•)
    Bonds fade slower, decreasing by one level every 3 months without
    reinforcement.

  - Bondslave (••)
    Becomes Blood Bonded to another Kindred with just one drink, not three.

  - Two Masters (•)  [Supernatural — from Blood Stained Love]
    Can be Blood Bound to two individuals simultaneously.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

FEEDING
Related to the act of feeding. Taking one does not require taking the others.

  MERITS
  - Bloodhound (•)
    Can sniff out resonances without tasting the blood.

  - Iron Gullet (•••)
    Can consume rancid, defractionated, or otherwise undrinkable blood.

  - Vessel Recognition (•)
    With a Resolve + Awareness test (Difficulty 2), can tell if a mortal has
    been fed on recently. A critical win reveals if the feeding is recurring
    (possible herd member).

  - Drive-thru (•)
    Has mastered feeding on the move. Can complete a hunt safely within minutes
    by increasing the difficulty by 1.

  FLAWS
  - Prey Exclusion (•)
    Cannot feed from a specific group. Takes Stains as if breaking a Chronicle
    Tenet when doing so.

  - Methuselah's Thirst (•)
    Hunger can only be slaked to 0 by supernatural blood.

  - Farmer (••)
    Must spend 2 Willpower Points to feed on human blood. Ventrue may not take this.

  - Organovore (••)
    Can only slake Hunger by consuming human flesh and organs.

  - Vein Tapper (•)
    Finds feeding to be deeply personal. Goes out of their way to feed from
    unaware, drugged, or unconscious victims.

  - Outdated Preference (••)
    Must either capture and force mortals to adhere to their specific preference,
    or always spend 1 Willpower to feed.

  - Resonance Sensitivity (•)
    One specific resonance triggers a unique compulsion in the character.

  - Resonance Mimic (••)
    Gets influenced and penalized by the memories of their victim.

  - Sloppy Feeder (••)
    Feeding pattern is distinctive enough to be identified. One attack can be
    forensically linked to previous attacks.

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

MYTHIC
Related to vampire mythos.

  MERITS
  - Eat Food (••)
    Can consume food (no nourishment). Must expel it before resting for the day.

  - Cold Dead Hunger (•••)
    Add two dice to resist Hunger frenzy.

  - Pack Diablerie (••)
    Always absorbs the soul during Diablerie unless choosing otherwise.
    When assisting another in Diablerie, gains 5 XP to spend as if having
    committed it personally.

  - Luck of the Devil (••••)
    Once per session, when misfortune occurs, redirect it toward someone close
    to the character for them to take the fall instead.

  - Nuit Mode (••)
    Body does not revert to death-state each night. Can keep new haircuts and
    body modifications. Can undo changes as if mending Aggravated damage.
    Does not work for characters with Blood Potency higher than 1.

  - Object of Power (• – •••)
    Rare, powerful item with escalating benefits:
    • Reroll one die per story (excluding Hunger dice).
    •• Gain one bonus die to all Level 1 Ritual tests.
    ••• Receive a free premonition warning once per session when someone
        intends to cause harm.

  - Ley Line Leach (•)
    Follows ancient paths of power while traveling. After spending several hours
    traveling to a new city or locale, negate the need for a Rouse Check for
    the next night.

  - Persistent Blush (•••)
    A single activation of Blush of Life lasts 1 week.

  - Corpse Flesh (•)
    Unable to use Blush of Life.

  FLAWS
  - Folkloric Bane (•)
    Takes Aggravated Damage when touching a specific object rooted in vampire
    mythos (e.g., silver).

  - Folkloric Block (•)
    Must spend Willpower or move away from a specific object vampires are known
    to fear in mythos (e.g., holy symbols).

  - Stigmata (•)
    Bleeds from wounds on the hands, feet, and forehead when at Hunger 4.

  - Stake Bait (••)
    When staked, the character meets Final Death.

  - Starving Decay (••)
    When Hunger is 3 or higher, body shrivels and decays. Take a two-dice
    penalty to Physical tests and social interactions with mortals. Can risk
    the Masquerade.

  - Twice Cursed (••)
    Take the clan's variant Bane in addition to the regular Bane. Storyteller
    may prohibit this if the second Bane wouldn't fit the chronicle.

  - Land Locked (•)
    Cannot leave the land. Must make a Fear Frenzy test (Difficulty 3) to
    board a boat or plane.

  - Resistant Blush (•)
    When rolling a Rouse Check for Blush of Life, roll twice and take the
    lowest result.
